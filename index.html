About Me

At the age of 24, I am many things. I am a son, a brother, an employee, a musician, a student, a teacher, and many others. Today, I am a person who is currently in a turning point of his life.

For the past year, I have been working at a healthcare company called ElderServe Health, Inc. We are an MLTC that works like a nursing home and insurance agency, but lets you live in the comfort of your own home. I work in the financial department, where I started doing data entry and some analysis via Microsoft Excel and our own Web Application dealing with medical records. I then worked on the entirely new UAS system which is also a Web Application run by New York State to register and maintain records of all New York State residents who receive Medicaid benefits and are enrolled in various plans.

Over the last few months, it has occured to me that the Financial World is not where I want to be. I then decided to look for some new options of what I can do with the rest of my life. That's when I stumbled upon an idea.

I was talking to a friend of mine, who has always been really into computers and was one of those boy-geniuses you hear about cracking into the school mainframe to steal passwords so we can play virutal mini-golf in the school library, and I told him that I'm looking to make a career change. He said, "To where?" I gave him a shrug and told him I'm not sure, but it's stressing me out. Thats when he suggested to look into computers. Up until that point, I never really took computers seriously as a profession. I don't know why I never gave it much thought, considering I always liked computers and I had a talent for always getting the results I wanted from them, but I just didn't. So I started to probe around and find out more information.

I started talking to a few people while checking out a few websites. I then found a website called Code Academy and started the JavaScript track to get a feel for coding to see what I was getting myself into. After that first day, I was hooked. Every time I had some time to myself with my computer, I logged-on to learn something new. It became something fun for me, and I was looking forward to the next time I could sit infront of the computer and learn to make new things, as well as make them look better and perform different tasks.

A week or two goes by, and I then stumble upon an amazing opportunity. A school in my area is not only teaching a stack of languages to help me get started, but its an intensive one-stop-shop that will get me to the level of an entry-level developer by graduation. The kicker is, the whole class only takes 12 weeks. I have heard of there being trade-school type environments for programming where it's not necessary to go after a four year computer science degree, but for one class to really teach an entire stack at once is not only appealing, but it became a must have for me. And that brings me to where I am today. Writing my first webpage to win over the admissions board to advance to the next step in hopes to be accepted.
